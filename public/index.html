With many differing rules and decade-long attempts by taxing authorities to establish “streamlined” uniformity, compliance with sales and use tax laws demands business resources and can cause unforeseen budget woes.

Correct payment and collection of sales and use taxes, especially for an organization that spans multiple states and taxing jurisdictions, can be a significant challenge for any business.

Complicating factors include the ever-increasing taxation of non-traditional items such as services, software and maintenance agreements, to the proper tax calculations, credits or exemptions to claim on traditional items such as machinery, equipment and supplies. Other challenges facing tax planners include statutory tax refund provisions in lieu of up-front exemptions or the proper calculation of tax offsets to apply against sales or use tax audit assessments.

With over 50 years of multi-state and local tax experience, Brown Taxes’ professionals have the experience necessary to effectively minimize state and local tax burdens. Our senior level tax experts work directly with clients on every engagement to maintain a high level of personal attention and professionalism often unparalleled by larger firms.

Brown Taxes, LLC provides clients with a comprehensive review program designed to identify and correct consistent misapplications of sales and use tax laws.

When a review identifies material overpayments, Brown Taxes prepares all applicable refund claims. When a review identifies material underpayments, Brown Taxes can provide the guidance necessary to mitigate liabilities and minimize future exposures.

Brown Taxes’ review programs provide both retroactive and prospective benefits to clients. Retroactively through the recovery of available tax refunds and prospectively through up-to-date training programs designed for the personnel involved in making day-to-day sales and use tax determinations.

Our professionals maintain continued awareness of new and changing laws and regulations and the court decisions interpreting existing laws and regulations. This awareness ensures that our professionals perform an informed review and maximize available tax savings – savings that go right to our clients’ bottom line.

Susan Nelson-Brown
Director, State and Local Tax Services

Susan Nelson-Brown manages the Company’s Sales and Use Tax Division. In this capacity, she is responsible for developing tax planning strategies to assist clients in achieving current and future tax savings. She has worked for a number of major manufacturing clients to review audit assessments, file and monitor refund claims with various taxing jurisdictions and vendors, and negotiate favorable tax positions with taxing authorities. Her wide scope of services to clients includes providing reports detailing review procedures, findings, and recommendations; follow-up training of accounting staff; and alerting them of changes in regulations.

Active in the state and local tax field since 1978 when she joined the Illinois Department of Revenue as a revenue auditor. Subsequent to that she held the same position for the Massachusetts Department of Revenue and became a district manager for DuCharme, McMillen and Associates. Susan then formed her own company which she managed successfully for almost ten years before merging it with Smart & Associates, LLP (F/K/A Strategis) in 1999, which spun-off her practice area in 2006 to Brown and Associates, LLC.

Susan is a graduate of Illinois State University with a Bachelor of Science in Accounting. She has also completed various classes conducted by state taxing authorities and tax organizations. Susan serves on the Illinois State University Alumni Association Board of Directors. On the Alumni Board, she serves on several committees and chairs the Alumni Awards Committee.

As a member of the Institute for Professionals in Taxation, Susan served the Membership Admissions Committee as Chairman, and currently serves on the DFW Local Luncheon Committee. She also actively participates in the Federation of Tax Administrators, Southeastern Association of Tax Administrators, Western States Association of Tax Administrators, and Midwestern States Association of Tax Administrators.

Susan has worked on behalf of clients with sales and use tax issues in every state. As a result, she has extensive experience in dealing with auditors regarding the entire spectrum of audit problems and procedures.

Susan has been invited to speaking engagements regarding sales and use tax issues before the North Carolina Certified Public Accountants Members in Industry Conference, the Minneapolis Institute of Management Accountants, the North Georgia Chapter of Management Accountants, as well as other industry seminars.

Kevin R. Blake, CPA, MST
Director, State and Local Tax Services

Kevin Blake has extensive expertise in both multi-state and local sales and use tax issues. His responsibilities include research and planning, compliance reviews, overpayment reviews, voluntary disclosures, client representation during audits and appeals, litigation support, and preparation/presentation of procedural manuals and training seminars. His experience involves a number of diverse industries including aerospace, construction, graphic arts, manufacturing, non-profit, real estate, retail/wholesale, service, and telecommunications.

Kevin began his career in tax consulting in 1989 with DuCharme, McMillen and Associates, Inc. He joined American Express Tax and Business Services, Inc. (F/K/A Altschuler, Melvoin & Glasser LLP) in 1990 where he served as Director of Sales and Use Tax Services. He later joined Smart and Associates, LLP (F/K/A Strategis) in 1999, which spun-off his practice area in 2006 to Brown Taxes, LLC.

With over 25 years of consulting, Kevin has represented clients with sales and use tax issues in many taxing jurisdictions, including: Alabama, Arizona, California, Chicago, Colorado, Connecticut, DC, Florida, Georgia, Hawaii, Illinois, Indiana, Iowa, Kentucky, Maryland, Minnesota, Missouri, New Jersey, New York, North Carolina, North Dakota, Ohio, Pennsylvania, South Carolina, Tennessee, Texas, Utah, Virginia, Washington and Wisconsin.

Kevin has participated in speaking engagements on various topics of taxation, including “Illinois Service Occupation Taxes” on the Accountability Program on WFBT- Channel 23, “The Impact of Illinois Sales/Use Tax Laws on Service Occupations” at the Midwest Accounting & Business Show, and the “Mulitstate Tax Half Day Seminar” for the Illinois CPA Society. He has also authored numerous articles on sales and use tax issues in professional journals such as Manu-Facts, Distribution Focus, Lawyer’s Business Journal, and The Construction Industry Advisor.
